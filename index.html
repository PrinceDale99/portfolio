  • Documentary

    Kwitis

    Kwitis is a gripping documentary by Marion Derama, Prince Dale Limosnero, and their friends, following a group of friends as they explore the night sky and launch a "kwitis rocket." The film not only captures the thrill of the moment but also delves into the darker side of the Philippines at night. The group encounters a dangerous situation when they meet the rugby boys, who forcefully take one of their kwitis rockets. Kwitis explores the excitement, risk, and unpredictability of the night, offering a raw look at both the beauty and dangers lurking in the shadows.

  • Social Realism, Drama, Psychological

    2023

    The Unravelled Dreams

    The Unraveled Dreams follows the story of Stan, a young man from a financially struggling and abusive family. After enduring years of abuse at the hands of his father, Stan makes the difficult decision to leave home. Influenced by his friends, he turns to selling drugs on the street to survive. However, his world is shaken when he receives a call from his sister, Missy, informing him that their mother is in the hospital. As Stan grapples with the overwhelming weight of his circumstances, the film explores themes of family, loss, and the harsh realities of life in poverty. The Unraveled Dreams digs into the choices Stan makes, the consequences of those choices, and the emotional toll of a life shaped by hardship.

    BASILIO

    Basilio is a thought-provoking film that offers a fresh perspective on the iconic character from José Rizal's El Filibusterismo. Told through Basilio's eyes, the story explores his inner struggles, personal growth, and the harsh realities of a corrupt society. The film explores his journey from a young, idealistic man to someone shaped by the injustices he witnesses, highlighting his emotional and intellectual transformation. As Basilio grapples with the weight of his experiences, the narrative uncovers the complexities of rebellion, sacrifice, and the search for justice. With a blend of historical drama and psychological depth, Basilio is a powerful reflection on the enduring impact of social and political systems.
